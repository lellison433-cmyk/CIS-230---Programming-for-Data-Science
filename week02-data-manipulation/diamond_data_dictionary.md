## The Diamonds Dataset

The Diamonds dataset contains information on **53,940 diamonds** and is commonly used for data exploration, visualization, and machine learning.

The dataset includes measurements of a diamond's size, quality, and price.

**Original Dataset:** https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/shivam2503/diamonds

### Variables

#### Price

* **price** (Numerical)
* Price of the diamond in U.S. dollars.

#### Carat

* **carat** (Numerical)
* Weight of the diamond.
* One carat is equal to 200 milligrams.
* Larger diamonds typically have higher prices.

#### Cut

* **cut** (Ordinal Categorical)
* Measures the quality of the diamond's cut.
* Better cuts generally reflect light more effectively.

#### Order (lowest to highest quality):

1. Fair
2. Good
3. Very Good
4. Premium
5. Ideal

#### Color

* **color** (Ordinal Categorical)
* Measures how colorless the diamond is.
* More colorless diamonds are generally considered higher quality.

Order (highest to lowest quality):

1. D (most colorless)
2. E
3. F
4. G
5. H
6. I
7. J (most visible color tint)

#### Clarity

* **clarity** (Ordinal Categorical)
* Measures the number, size, and visibility of imperfections within a diamond.
* Internal imperfections are called *inclusions*.
* Surface imperfections are called *blemishes*.

Order (lowest to highest quality):

1. I1 – Included 1
2. SI2 – Slightly Included 2
3. SI1 – Slightly Included 1
4. VS2 – Very Slightly Included 2
5. VS1 – Very Slightly Included 1
6. VVS2 – Very Very Slightly Included 2
7. VVS1 – Very Very Slightly Included 1
8. IF – Internally Flawless

#### Depth

* **depth** (Numerical)
* Total depth percentage of the diamond.
* Represents the height of the diamond relative to its width.

#### Table

* **table** (Numerical)
* Table percentage of the diamond.
* Represents the size of the flat top surface relative to the diamond's width.

#### Physical Dimensions

* **x** (Numerical)

  * Length of the diamond in millimeters.

* **y** (Numerical)

  * Width of the diamond in millimeters.

* **z** (Numerical)

  * Height of the diamond in millimeters.
